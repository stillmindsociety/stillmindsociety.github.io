Mindful Spaces Project

Status: Active Research & Prototyping

Timeline: 18-month project (6 months completed)

Project Overview

The Mindful Spaces Project challenges the assumption that productive environments must be stimulating environments. We're designing and testing spaces—both physical and digital—that support deep work, creative thinking, and genuine human connection.

Current Activities

  • • Environmental psychology research
  • • Biometric testing of stress responses in different spaces
  • • Prototype "calm room" digital interfaces
  • • Partnership development with coworking spaces
  • • User interviews with remote workers and digital nomads

Expected Outcomes

  • • Open-source design toolkit for contemplative environments
  • • Research paper on environmental factors and cognitive performance
  • • Pilot installation in partner coworking space
  • • Digital interface patterns for calm-inducing apps

How to get involved: We're seeking partner organizations, user research participants, and funding for environmental testing equipment.

Attention Economics Research

Status: Active Data Collection

Timeline: 2-year longitudinal study (8 months completed)

Research Questions

  • • What is the true cognitive cost of task-switching and notification interruptions?
  • • How does constant connectivity affect creative problem-solving abilities?
  • • What are the long-term mental health implications of attention fragmentation?
  • • How do different demographics experience and cope with digital overwhelm?

Methodology

We're conducting mixed-methods research including cognitive testing, diary studies, biometric monitoring, and in-depth interviews with knowledge workers across various industries.

Preliminary Findings

  • • 73% of participants underestimate their daily notification count
  • • Creative problem-solving scores improve by 34% after 48-hour digital breaks
  • • Attention residue persists for an average of 23 minutes after interruption

How to get involved: We're recruiting study participants and seeking institutional research partnerships.

Boredom Revival Campaign

Status: Planning & Strategy Development

Launch Date: Q2 2025

Campaign Vision

A cultural movement to rebrand boredom from a problem to be solved into a skill to be developed. We're creating content, experiences, and community around the radical act of doing nothing productive.

Planned Activities

  • • "Boredom Bootcamp" workshop series
  • • Social media campaign celebrating unstimulated moments
  • • Partnership with meditation and mindfulness organizations
  • • Academic research collaboration on boredom and creativity
  • • Pop-up "Nothing Rooms" in busy urban areas

Target Outcomes

  • • Shift cultural narrative around productivity and rest
  • • Provide practical tools for embracing unstimulated time
  • • Build community around intentional living
  • • Influence policy discussions around right to disconnect

How to get involved: We're seeking campaign partners, workshop venues, and community organizers passionate about reclaiming downtime.

Digital Detox Toolkit

Status: Development Phase

Beta Release: Spring 2025

Toolkit Components

  • • Progressive disconnection guides (no cold turkey required)
  • • Notification audit and optimization templates
  • • Alternative activity suggestions for former scrolling time
  • • Family and workplace boundary-setting frameworks
  • • Measurement tools for attention quality and recovery

Design Philosophy

Unlike all-or-nothing digital detox approaches, our toolkit emphasizes sustainable, incremental changes that work with human psychology rather than against it. We focus on intentional engagement rather than total abstinence.

Target Users

  • • Knowledge workers feeling overwhelmed by digital demands
  • • Parents wanting to model healthy tech relationships
  • • Students struggling with focus and attention
  • • Organizations implementing digital wellness programs

How to get involved: We're recruiting beta testers and seeking partnerships with wellness organizations and HR departments.

Contemplative Gaming

Status: Prototype Development

First Release: Q3 2025

Game Design Principles

  • • Slow thinking over fast reactions
  • • Reflection prompts built into gameplay
  • • Natural stopping points and play session limits
  • • No engagement dark patterns or addiction mechanics
  • • Integration with real-world mindfulness practices

Current Prototypes

  • • Pause: A puzzle game where solutions emerge through waiting
  • • Breathe: Rhythm game synchronized to breathing patterns
  • • Wander: Open-world exploration with no objectives or achievements

Research Partnership

We're collaborating with cognitive psychology researchers to measure the effects of contemplative game mechanics on attention, stress, and creative thinking.

How to get involved: We're seeking playtesting volunteers and indie game developers interested in ethical design patterns.

Stillness in Motion

Status: Concept Development

Timeline: Seeking funding and venue partnerships

Installation Concept

Interactive art pieces that explore the paradox of finding calm within movement. Visitors engage with motion-sensing installations that respond to intentional, mindful movement while filtering out anxious or distracted motion.

Planned Installations

  • • Breathing Walls: Light installations that sync to collective breathing patterns
  • • Intention Mirrors: Reflective surfaces that only show clear images during mindful movement
  • • Soundscape Gardens: Audio environments that evolve based on visitor presence and stillness

Educational Component

Each installation includes guided experiences exploring themes of attention, intention, and the relationship between external motion and internal stillness.

How to get involved: We're seeking gallery partnerships, technical collaborators, and funding for prototype development.
